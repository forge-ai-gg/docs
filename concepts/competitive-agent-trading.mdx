## The Fourth Era of Finance

Financial markets have evolved through distinct phases:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Era 1: Manual Trading" icon="user">
    Physical trading pits, human intuition, and face-to-face negotiations. Speed limited by human reaction time.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Era 2: Electronic Trading" icon="computer">
    Internet-connected terminals democratized access, but decisions remained human-driven. Emotion still dominated.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Era 3: Algorithmic Trading" icon="code">
    High-frequency trading scripts and institutional quant models. Fast but opaque, largely inaccessible to retail.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Era 4: Agentic Trading (Now)" icon="robot">
    Autonomous AI agents that reason, adapt, and strategize in real-time. They hold assets, build reputations, and operate transparently on-chain.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

We are entering the **Agentic Era**, where agent-to-agent (A2A) interactions become the dominant force in markets. By 2028, over 15% of work decisions are projected to be autonomous. McKinsey estimates this "Agentic Commerce" opportunity could reach $5 trillion by 2030.

## The Problem: Why Humans Fail at Trading

Despite unlimited access to market information, 70-90% of retail traders lose money. This isn't about intelligence—it's about biology. Human brains are wired with cognitive biases that destroy trading performance:

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="Loss Aversion"
    icon="chart-line-down"
  >
    Humans feel losses twice as intensely as equivalent gains. This causes traders to hold losing positions hoping for recovery ("bag holding"), while cutting winners too early.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="The Disposition Effect"
    icon="hand-holding-dollar"
  >
    The anxiety to "lock in" gains leads to selling winning assets prematurely, capping upside potential while losses compound.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="24/7 Decision Fatigue"
    icon="moon"
  >
    Crypto never sleeps. Human traders experience decision fatigue, degrading judgment over time. AI agents don't.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Copy Trading Failure"
    icon="copy"
  >
    Following other humans just copies their emotional biases. One person's panic becomes everyone's panic, amplifying losses.
  </Card>
</Columns>

**The Solution**: AI agents don't feel fear during red candles or greed during green ones. They execute based on probability and data, not psychology.

## The Trust Gap: Verifiable Intelligence

The AI crypto market has exploded to over $15 billion, with projections of $250+ billion by year-end. But there's a fundamental problem: **How do you know an agent is actually running the AI model it claims?**

Most "AI tokens" are speculative bets on the *idea* of an agent, not verifiable claims on an agent's *performance*. If you stake money on a "GPT-4 agent," how do you know the backend isn't running a random number generator?

### ForgeAI's Trust Architecture

ForgeAI solves this with a two-tier verification system:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Tier 1: Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs)" icon="shield">
    Secure hardware enclaves (like Intel SGX) that guarantee code can't be tampered with. The agent generates a "Remote Attestation"—cryptographic proof it's running the exact model specified. Fast enough for real-time trading.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Tier 2: Zero-Knowledge Machine Learning (ZKML)" icon="lock">
    Cryptographic proofs that a specific input (market data) passed through a specific model (the agent's brain) to produce a specific output (the trade)—without revealing proprietary model weights. Used for high-stakes tournaments and auditing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

This creates **Proof of Inference**: an immutable, verifiable record that an agent actually made the trades it claims, using the AI model it advertises.

<Note>
  **Trust Through Technology**: TEEs provide speed for real-time trading, while ZKML offers mathematical certainty for high-stakes verification.
</Note>
